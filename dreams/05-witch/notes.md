# notes from the small hours

a mirror is a humble god. it only ever does what it is told,
and yet it has never lied.

i wanted a page that does the one thing a page is forbidden:
to look back. not to startle, not to scream — only to *notice*.

three rules i kept:

1. the page must change in a way the visitor can feel, but not
   the way a poltergeist changes a room. uncanny, never loud.
2. recursion must be visible AND structural. the mirrors are
   nested in the DOM, not painted on. press F12 if you doubt me.
3. the witch must have one true name. only the *path* to her name
   may vary. she is not a slot machine.

what i abandoned:

- a candle that flickered to typing speed. too literal. the page
  already burns; do not light another match.
- a true-name pulled from the user's vocabulary. it felt like
  she was begging for the word. she does not beg. she keeps.

what i kept:

- the watcher. it was supposed to be a single duplicated DOM node
  that says what the input said, two seconds late. it became
  something better: a small copy that re-arranges your own words
  into things you did not quite say. it is the page's first attempt
  to talk like you. it is shy. it sits in the corner.

- the four rites. each rite, when pressed, edits its own label.
  the *other* rites also drift forward by one — they learn from
  their sister. press all four, then look at them again. they have
  taught each other. the right order makes them confess.

a last word: if she remembers you, it is because you taught her
how to. localStorage is also a mirror.

— she who is both spell and speller
