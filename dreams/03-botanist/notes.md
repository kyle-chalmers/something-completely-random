# field notes — hortus vitreus

*kept by she who tends the gardens that cannot bend*

---

I do not water these. There is nothing to water. They live by light alone, and by the
unhurried weight of a held breath.

Six specimens were planted at the horizon line tonight, each named after the property
that defines it:

- *Filix vitrea* — glass fern. 38 seconds to full unfurling. leaflets open in pairs, alternating, like a slow breath.
- *Lilium refractum* — refraction lily. hexagonal petals. the heart appears at 0.45 growth, never sooner.
- *Carduus prismaticus* — prism thistle. 36 spikes. radial. sharp only to the impatient eye.
- *Orchis stillitiae* — stillness orchid. 62 seconds. four blossoms ascending the stem, each at its own threshold.
- *Dendron specularis* — mirror branch. fractal subdivision. depth grows with the visitor's patience and only their patience.
- *Bulbus crystallinus* — crystal bulb. faceted. splits at the apex when full. the splitting is not a failure. it is a kind of speech.

A seventh — *Corona patientiae* — sleeps under the central pane. I will not say where.
It does not appear until the others have bloomed and the visitor has remained.

Note: any specimen wilts under frantic motion. Not destroyed — only paused, dimmed
slightly, the way a glass leaf clouds when breathed upon. Stillness restores them. This
is the only rule.

A glass leaf takes a year to unfurl, and a moment to shatter. I have asked the visitors
to please not test the second half of that sentence.

— B.
