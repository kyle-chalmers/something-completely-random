# Engineer's Logbook · Plate № 887.γ

*9 may 2026 — workshop, the small hours*

The patent office returned the Calibration Engine for the fourth time today, with a polite note suggesting that a flywheel which "has not stopped since 1887" is a category error rather than a mechanism. They are not wrong. They are not helpful.

**The belt.** The belt crosses itself exactly once and is not twisted. I will not be drawn on this. Two examiners have already declined to look at it twice. I have removed the second crossing and the apparatus continues to function, which suggests the second crossing was decorative.

**Gear-2 ratio.** Set at 1 : φ deliberately. An output that never returns to its origin cannot be cycled, and a mechanism that cannot be cycled cannot, strictly speaking, be perpetual. By calling it perpetual anyway I avoid having to prove the negative. (See appendix γ, omitted on grounds of relevance.)

**The vent (T).** Draws steam inward rather than expelling it. This was not intended; I observed it on Thursday and have built the rest of the machine around the observation. The vent is the engine. The vent has always been the engine. The pistons and bearings are decorative — a courtesy to anyone who needs to see a piston before they will believe in motion.

**The shilling.** Disassembly is reversible if the operator does not stop. If the operator stops, the shilling settles into whichever configuration was most recent. This is the only honest part of the device.

**The escapement.** Beats once per second. It drives nothing. It is included because a clockwork apparatus without a tick is suspicious. The tick is the proof.

**Net work done.** Goes negative under load. The engine returns work to the operator. I am not yet sure where the work comes from. Possibly the operator's certainty. Possibly the workshop. Possibly 1887.

**Outstanding tasks:**
- Draw the third gear, or do not. (Have decided: do not.)
- Reply to patent office. (Have decided: do not.)
- Determine whether the Certificate of Calibration is a reward or a confession. (Inconclusive after seven and one-half turns.)

— *the engineer of impossible machines*
