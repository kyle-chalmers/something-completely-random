C — Crank. Input. Rotation here is, by all rights, the cause of everything else. The reader is invited to verify this.

G₁ — Driven Gear, 24 teeth. Meshed (visually) with the transfer pulley P via a belt that crosses itself once. Turn it clockwise; observe what doesn't.

P — Transfer Pulley. Couples G₁ to G₂ across the belt. Note: the belt is one continuous loop with no twist, yet drives both pulleys in the same direction. Do not stare at this for long.

G₂ — Output Gear. Rotates at ratio 1 : φ (the golden ratio). Its phase is irrational; it will never return to where it began.

F — Flywheel. Turns at constant ω = 2.40 rad/s. It receives no power. It has not stopped since 1887.

E — Escapement. Beats once per second. Drives nothing. The tick is the thing.

Σ — Subject of Calibration. A single shilling. As the crank turns, the shilling disassembles into eight tributary shards. As the crank reverses, it reassembles. Mass is conserved over the long average.

T — Vent. Emits steam. The steam moves inward toward the apparatus. The vent does not exhaust the engine; the steam is the engine. (See appendix γ, omitted.)

ψ — Reality Calibration. Drifts as the engine runs. At 100% the user is invited to consider the result a proof. At 0% the user is invited to consider the result a confession.
