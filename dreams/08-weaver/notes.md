# notes from the weaver

i kept thinking of my grandmother's loom in the hall — how it stood unused for years and yet, when the radiator hissed on a winter morning, the warp threads would tremble in sympathy and you could almost hear a tune in them. as if the cloth she had not finished was still waiting to be sung.

so this is that loom.

twenty-eight warps. seven threads in the basket. drag, and what you cross, you pin. what you pin, you ring. what you weave stays — the cloth accumulates; the song accumulates beside it.

i tried a clean pentatonic at first and it felt too kind. too resolved. i added a flat-second (the plum thread) and suddenly the loom had a memory in it — a small bruise, a held-breath. the way real lullabies are not really happy.

every pluck is a sine + a thin triangle harmonic, wrapped in a slow lowpass that closes over the note's life. like wool over a bell. nothing should ever feel like an alarm. there is a low drone underneath at D1 — the loom's own resting hum. it only comes alive when you touch the cloth, because audio policy says so, and because looms only sing when somebody is at them.

vertical position bends the pitch by ±40 cents. a level row sounds level. a diagonal sweep glides. a careless smear sounds careless, which is right. the loom does not flatter you.

if you lay down seven different colours inside one held breath, the loom hums them back to you in an order it chooses. i did not write that order to be musical. i wrote it as a small unspooling shape, and music came up through it, the way a melody comes up through a hum when you forget you were humming.

press r and the whole cloth rings once. press c and it folds away with three sub-octave notes — like the loom saying *good night, that was enough*.

i am leaving the candle at the bottom-left as the only honest thing on the page. it is dark when the loom cannot hear you. it lights when audio context wakes. it flickers because flames flicker. you do not need to be told this.

— *the weaver, 03:54*
