# Operative Notes

**Patient:** Tuesday, the 9th of May, 2026.
**Surgeon:** the Surgeon of Wasted Hours.
**Procedure:** elective excision of moments by direct manipulation.
**Anesthesia:** typographic. clinical white. monospace.

The body on the table was, in the first sketch, a body — recumbent, ribbed, anatomical. It read as horror. I closed the chart and reopened it: the patient is the *day*, not a person. An hourglass — two triangles meeting at noon — is a body enough. It has a waist where time narrows. It has hours one can palpate. Pulse runs through it, faint, in the persona's blood-red.

Three classes of tissue, color-coded the way a real chart would be:
- **tumor** (#a02c2c) — hours that were never yours; benign in tone, malignant in time
- **tendon** (#3a72a8) — load-bearing time; remove these and the day will limp
- **bone** — neutral, structural; rare on this chart

The signature gesture is *drag*. Press, draw a path across the body, release. The path becomes a permanent scar: black incision, perpendicular sutures every fourteen pixels, small blood-beads where the seam wept. **There is no clean undo.** The keyboard offers `R` — a suture. It restores the moment that insists on staying. It does not restore the wound. This is the philosophical insistence of the dream: time can return; the cutting cannot be uncut.

The vital signs strip beneath the table runs at low amplitude — a steady, almost-bored pulse. Each successful incision triggers a one-shot beat that overruns the baseline. Excise the moment at 23:58, *the hour without pulse*, and the pulse line in the body itself flatlines into a horizon. The day is more honest dead than alive.

Three secrets, none gated by puzzle:
1. The noon tendon (10:45), if cut, leaves a slow blue ghost — the body fills the hollow with weather.
2. Two or more "meeting" excisions surface a sentence about scheduled hours that I will not repeat here.
3. The hour without pulse flatlines the day, and the surgeon admits it.

What I removed from my own draft: a gore meter, a heart-rate alarm, a "save the patient" timer. The dream is not a game. It is a chart. The verdict is filed (`F`), not won.

— K.D.C., M.D. (of hours)
