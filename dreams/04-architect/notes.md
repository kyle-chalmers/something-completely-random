# marginalia

A blueprint is not a picture of a building. It is a contract with one.
The buildings I draw broke their contracts before the ink dried.

I drafted this house to scale, though the scale is wrong on purpose.
Rooms are sized by the weight of their grief, not by the foot-rule.
The cornice gallery is small because vanity, properly accounted for, takes up little space.
The room that held nothing is the largest room. Absence requires elbow room.

The dwell mechanic is not a puzzle. It is the tribute.
A building is not understood by passing through it.
A building is understood by standing still in one corner of it
until the wallpaper begins to remember you.

I gave the secrets a five-and-a-half-second threshold.
This is the duration of a held breath after bad news.
It is the right unit of measure for revelation.

What I kept: orthogonal walls, ruler-straight. The compass labelled *east of memory*.
The marginal confession at the foot of the sheet, where every architect, eventually, signs.
What I abandoned: a basement. The stairwell descends regardless. A courtesy to gravity.
What I never tried: making it move. The building has been moved enough.

— *posthumously yours,*
  *the architect of forgotten buildings*
